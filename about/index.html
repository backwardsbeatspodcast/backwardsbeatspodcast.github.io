About Backwards Beats Podcast

Welcome to Backwards Beats, the podcast where we dive deep into the iconic albums that shaped music history. Hosted by Carl Pfanstiehl and Dan Woodard, we're on a mission to explore and review the Rolling Stone’s 500 Greatest Albums of All Time, one episode at a time.

What We Do

Each week, we turn back the musical clock and rediscover the magic, the melodies, and the stories behind some of the most influential albums ever made. From classic rock to soul, jazz, and beyond, we provide fresh takes and honest opinions on the albums we are presented.

In each episode, you'll find:

  • Track-by-track reviews with a breakdown of standout songs.
  • Personal takes on whether these albums truly deserve their place in the top 500.
  • Segments like “Quality Time”, where we dissect a single track, and “Would You Hit It?”, where we decide if the album we would hit it and add it to our vinyl collection.
  • Plenty of humor, amazing sponsors, and a touch of parody to keep things fun.

We’re here for everyone from die-hard music fans to casual listeners looking to expand their musical palate. Whether you're rediscovering old favorites or exploring new genres, we hope to inform and entertain and that you'll come along on this journey with us.

Why Listen?

If you love music, music history, thoughtful (and often funny) commentary along with a little music theory, and want to join us on this journey through the "greatest albums ever made", Backwards Beats is for you.

Meet the Hosts

Carl Pfanstiehl and Dan Woodard are lifelong friends with a shared love for music. Dan brings his technical background and attention to detail to the production side of things, while Carl’s passion for deep musical analysis keeps each episode rich with insights.

Together, they balance humor and honesty, giving you an entertaining and informative look at each album. Whether they agree or disagree, their dynamic makes for a lively discussion each week.

What’s Next?

We’re excited for what’s ahead! We’ve got more albums to explore, more sponsors (real and parody) to introduce, and plenty of music history to unpack. As we continue building this podcast, we look forward to expanding our community of listeners who love music just as much as we do.

Backwards Beats: It’s more than just music—it’s the stories, the impact, and the conversation. Come rewind with us.
