Wiki Summary

Suicide is the debut album from the American rock band Suicide. It was released in 1977 on Red Star Records and produced by Craig Leon and Marty Thau. The album was recorded in four days at Ultima Sound Studios in New York as the group already had practiced and created songs from their previous five years of music gigs. The album featured songs about urban life and people who vocalist Alan Vega knew in New York. Vega's vocals on the album were influenced by 1950s rock musicians such as Elvis Presley while the backing music was provided by Martin Rev Read more on Last.fm.

Dan's Notes

This record is considered very influential, providing a foundation of raw ingredients that have inspired many genres and artists. Alan Vega's haunting vocals and Martin Rev's relentless synth beats create a minimalist and intense sound, capturing the chaotic essence of urban rebellion. The album blends a punk punk energy with proto-electronic sounds.

Listening closely, I do hear some of the artists that they influenced. * Beats of Fury - Chemical Brothers * Closer - NIN * Death Valley 69 - Sonic Youth * Nightclubbing - Iggy Pop (the drum sound) Henry Rollins from Black Flag has sited suicide as an influence many times.

However, I must admit that this album represents a very tedious listen for me. It's like consuming baker's chocolate: while I can appreciate the raw ingredients and their potential, it's not something I'd choose to enjoy on its own. I love punk and some industrial music, but I prefer the polished, finished product over the raw components. This album, much like raw cacao, is more of a historical artifact than something I'd revisit for pleasure.

In fact, I wouldn't recommend any individual track from this album to a friend. While I recognize its importance and influence, the songs feel more suited as backing tracks in a psychedelic film, perhaps something directed by David Lynch, rather than as stand-alone pieces of art. The atmosphere they create is compelling, but not something I'd personally enjoy outside of that context.
